In web development, a cron job is basically a scheduled task that runs automatically at a specific time or interval on a server.

      Think of it as an alarm clock for your server that tells it:
      “Run this script at 2:00 AM every day” or
      “Check this every 5 minutes.”
            🔹 Where It’s Used in Web Development
            In real projects, cron jobs are very common. For example:

            Database Maintenance → Automatically back up your database every night at 12 AM.

            Email Notifications → Send reminder emails to users daily at 9 AM.

            Cache Clearing → Clean old cache files every week.

            Report Generation → Generate daily/weekly/monthly reports.

            Payment Systems → Retry failed payments or process subscriptions at scheduled times.

            Data Sync → Fetch or update data from an external API every hour.
